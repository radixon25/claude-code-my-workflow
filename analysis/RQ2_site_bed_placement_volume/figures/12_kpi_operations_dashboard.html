About This Dashboard
This dashboard shows purely descriptive placement KPIs — no statistical modeling or regression coefficients. It is designed for operational decision-making by leadership.

What “48-hour lag” means: Bed counts reflect the number of beds open at 9am two days before a placement is recorded. This accounts for the full operational pipeline: bed opens → candidate identified → submitted for approval → approved → client arrives → case closed in Salesforce. The typical pipeline takes 12–48 hours, so a 48-hour window captures the full cycle.

Key metrics:
• Placements per Bed-Day = Total placements ÷ total bed-days available (48h lag). The core efficiency metric: how well does each shelter convert available capacity into placements?
• Daily Rate = Average placements per day.
• Consistency = Percentage of days with at least one placement.
• Utilization = Approximate bed occupancy rate.
• Velocity = On days when placements do happen, how many occur? Higher = batch processing.

Performance Tiers are based on terciles of placements-per-bed among shelters with sufficient data (≥ 3 placements, ≥ 14 days). Shelters below these thresholds are marked “Insufficient Data.”

Excluded sites: EHARC and AIC are excluded because they do not participate in the TBC placement pipeline for singles and families.

Data: TBC placements from Salesforce matched to city bed tracker. Dashboard generated by site_placement_analysis.R
