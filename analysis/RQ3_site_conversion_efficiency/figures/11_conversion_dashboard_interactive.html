What is the Conversion Coefficient?

Each shelter gets a Poisson regression estimating: “When this shelter had one more bed open yesterday, how many additional placements did it make today?”

  • Positive coefficient (green) = more open beds → more placements. The shelter efficiently converts available capacity into placements.
  • Near-zero coefficient = bed availability does not predict placements at this site. Other factors (approval speed, client readiness, staffing) may be the bottleneck.
  • Negative coefficient (red) = more open beds correlate with fewer placements. This is counterintuitive and usually reflects low overall volume or operational issues rather than a causal effect.

The coefficient is in log-rate units. A coefficient of 0.30 means each additional bed increases the daily placement rate by about 35% (exp(0.30) ≈ 1.35).

Status Definitions

  • ✓ Reliable: Regression converged with a numerically stable estimate. Criteria: |coefficient| < 5, standard error < 5, and mean available beds ≥ 0.5. Use these for operational decision-making.
  • Unreliable estimate: Regression converged but produced extreme or numerically unstable results—typically because the shelter has near-zero beds on most days, very few placements, or both. Do not use for comparisons.
  • Insufficient data: Fewer than 2 total placements or fewer than 10 site-days in the analysis period. No regression was attempted. Monitor as more data accumulates.

How Demographic is Determined

Each shelter is categorized by all populations it serves based on the city’s bed tracker data. We identify every distinct population label (Men, Women, Youth, Families) across all bed sub-sites for each shelter group.

  • Men & Women: Shelter operates beds for both men and women (e.g., YWLA has YWLA-Men + YWLA-Women sub-sites)
  • Men: Shelter operates beds exclusively for men
  • Women: Shelter operates beds exclusively for women
  • Women & Families: Shelter operates beds for women and families
  • Youth: Shelter operates beds for youth
  • Families: Shelter operates beds exclusively for families

This approach captures multi-population shelters accurately rather than assigning a single majority label.

Methodology

  • Data: TBC placements from Salesforce matched to city bed tracker via a curated crosswalk (90% coverage of eligible placed cases).
  • Model: Site-specific Poisson regression: placements ~ beds_lag1 + temperature + cold_day for each shelter independently.
  • Lag: Uses yesterday’s 9am bed count (lag-1) to predict today’s placements, reflecting the ~36-hour operational pipeline from bed availability to case closure in Salesforce.
  • Controls: Average temperature and cold-day indicator (min < 20°F) control for weather-driven demand variation.
  • Period: 2026-01-10 through 2026-02-23 (44 days).

Technical Notes
Conversion coefficients are estimated via site-specific Poisson regressions using glm(n_placed ~ beds_lag1 + avg_temp_f + cold_day, family=poisson). The system-level Poisson FE model (pooling all sites with shelter + day-of-week fixed effects) yields a null pooled result (coef=-0.00144, p=0.0701), but site-specific models reveal substantial heterogeneity across shelters. The Women-only subgroup is statistically significant (p=0.0015).

Data pipeline: TBC work orders from Salesforce → parquet extract → crosswalk to city bed tracker site names → site-day panel (1494 rows, 34 shelters, 44 days).
